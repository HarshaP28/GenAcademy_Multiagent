[1:00 - ARCHITECTURE]
(show assets/architecture_diagram.png)
Let us walk through the architecture diagram to see how it works under the hood. (pause)
First, every query enters the Security Checkpoint node where we scrub PII and check for prompt injection threats.
Second, safe requests are routed to the Root Orchestrator Agent.
Third, the orchestrator delegates tasks to specialized sub-agents, which include the Traffic Agent, Healthcare Agent, Environment Agent, Emergency Agent, and Public Services Agent.
Fourth, these specialized sub-agents call tools on our local Model Context Protocol server, like get_traffic_conditions and get_environmental_data.
Fifth, their outputs are consolidated and passed to the Human Approval node, which pauses for administrator permission if critical safety alerts are detected.
Sixth, once approved, the Decision Intelligence Agent compiles the final structured report and formats it for output.
